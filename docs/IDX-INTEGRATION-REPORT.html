Section 05

Compliance & Legal

Displaying MLS listings on a website comes with rules from both Bright MLS and the National Association of Realtors. We handle all of this for you.

Here's what needs to appear on every listing page, and how we'll make sure it's done right:

✓
Listing Agent & Firm Credits Every listing shows the name of the listing agent and their brokerage, as required by MLS rules.
✓
Bright MLS Attribution The Bright MLS logo and copyright notice appear on all listing pages, meeting their branding requirements.
✓
Data Accuracy Disclaimers Standard disclaimers let visitors know the data comes from the MLS and should be verified independently.
✓
"Last Updated" Timestamps Every listing shows when the data was last refreshed, so visitors know they're seeing current information.
✓
Your Branding Comes First NAR rules require your company branding to be larger than any third-party logos. Your brand always leads.
✓
Opt-Out Compliance Some sellers choose not to have their listing shown on IDX sites. Our system automatically respects those preferences.

You don't need to worry about any of this. We build all required disclaimers, credits, and notices directly into the website templates. They show up automatically on every listing page. If Bright MLS updates their rules, we update the templates.

What We Need From You

To get started, we need a few items from you. Most of these you already have as a Bright MLS subscriber.

  • Bright MLS Subscriber ID — Your unique ID in the Bright MLS system.
  • NRDS Number — Your NAR member ID, found on your REALTOR membership card.
  • Brokerage Office Code — LYL Realty Group's office code in Bright MLS.
  • IDX Authorization — Authorize your website to display IDX data. We'll walk you through it.
  • Neighborhood Preferences — Which Philadelphia neighborhoods and zip codes to focus on.

Don't have all of this ready? No problem. We can help you track down each item. Most brokers get everything within a day or two.
